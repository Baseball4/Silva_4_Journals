Copyright - legal rights given to a creater to print, publish, perform, film, or record literacy, or musical material, and to
authorize others to do the same.

Copyleft - making that work free now and everytime its modified and or extentions are made.

Fair Use - doctrine that states copyright material can, under certain circumstances, be quoted verbatim for purposes
such as criticism, news reporting, teaching, and reasearch with no need for permission.

Free Speech - the right to speak without censorship or restraint by the government.

Free Software - gives the user the freedom to share. study and modify the software.

Freeware - programming offered at no cost.

Public Domain - available to the public as a whole and not subject to copyright.

File-sharing - sharing a document/file over the internet to others.

Piracy - unauthorized use or reproduction of someones work.

Plagiarism - taking someones ideas and saing they are your own.

Infringment - breaking a law (copyright law).

Proprietary - an owner or ownership.

Open Source - software made freely availible and can be redistributed and modified.
